CAMEMBERT FIX THIS CHRISTMAS 

Christmas wouldn’t be Christmas without some Camembert, or so the famous first line Little women goes…

This beautifully gooey baked cheese is a dinner party classic, and for good reason! Whether it’s served as a mini fondue on a grazing board ready for guests to tuck into with slices of toasted sourdough, an elite starter flavored with fresh rosemary and garlic, or a baked bits. The star of the tea show, it can take the humble cheese. Take the most vanilla of dinner plans and turn them into a festive feast.

If you’re looking for a way to reinvent the Camembert wheel this Christmas, you’ve come to the right place because CheeseTok has been good to us this year, with creative recipes to impress. From truffle-flavored creations to blanket pasta wheels and riders in cranberry and camembert pastry twists, consider this the only inspo to ensure you take home the crown for host with the most this Christmas.

BAKED CAMEMBERT WITH HONEY AND CHILLI

A classic throw-in-the-oven, minimal-effort dish is a busy host’s best friend, and this Chili and Honey Camembert is 10/10 as delicious as it is easy. Simply score your Camembert, add a few sprigs of rosemary and drizzle with honey and chili flakes before baking, and be ready to wow. Serve with a slice of toasted sourdough, crackers or fluffy focaccia and enjoy the most starter you’ll be thinking about for days afterwards. Find the recipe on Lindsey’s website here.

CAMEMBERT AND CRANBERRY TWIST

Want a little crunch with your Camembert? Enter camembert and cranberry twists, fully baked and ready to share ‘n’ tears in 20 minutes. To make, simply roll out your Jus-Rol pastry and cut into two circles. Spread your first circle with a generous layer of the spicy cranberry sauce, place your camembert wheel in the middle and then place your second layer of pastry on top. Pinch the edges with a fork to secure the layers and cut slices into the pastry ready to make your twists. Beat an egg and egg wash the whole pastry. Once done, turn each of your pieces into place and bake for 20 minutes until golden.

Cut off the top ready to sink and dig in! Kudos, pastry chef. If you want to add an aspect of ‘health’, serve with a side of crunchy crudites for a fresh palate cleanser between the puff pastry.
