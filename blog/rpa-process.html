Robotic Process Automation

What is an RPA?

Robiotic process is the process of automating business operations using robots (bots) to reduce human intervention. Confused? do not worry. When we move forward in this blog, I will clear all your doubts.

Let me break the RPA:

Robotic: Adding a machine that imitates human actions

Process: a step -by -step impulse that can complete a job

Automation: Human intervention is not

Keeping it in mind, now we explain the RPA.

Democracy of human actions is known as the RPA to perform human intervention measures without any human intervention.

How does RPA work?

Now that we know what the RPA is, let's see how it works with a simple example. We say, a group of candidates have tested the ability for a company that is hiring them. The rental team enters the test score in Excel sheet, with the names of the candidates and their email ID names.

Now, just imagine that email messages will be sent to these candidates, saying that they have been called for the next round of interviews.

Prior to the start of the RPA, these messages had to be sent manually by the team. But now, by extracting data from the RPA Excel sheet, comparing the candidates' scores with cut off, and sending automatic email messages to the next interview round, protects the team from this painful work. A handful of orders and one click. In this way manual interference is reduced to a large scale.

Clearly, RPA is a technology. This is not a programming language or tool. To implement this technology, we have many tools. Let's discuss them in the next part.

Why RPA:

In a business organization, there are many repeated works that are performed on a daily basis. All the useless work that a person does on a regular basis can be replaced by a robot that works on RPA technology. It follows exactly the actions that are described by human beings and complete the work of human intervention without any human intervention.

In this way, a user can be spent on repeatedly using those who use it for another important task.

For example, at the level of data entry, the data entry clerk visits the same source every day and receives data from there and fills it in Excel sheet. There is a set of steps prescribed in this process, such as visiting the website, extracting data from there, and adding it to the sheet. But, if a robot can only be trained to do so with some simple orders, then why not, okay?

The robot follows these orders and completes the work fast and more accurately.

It is one of the millions of tasks that the RPA can do. Most organizations use the RPA for everyday business tasks from the end to automation.
